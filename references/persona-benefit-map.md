# Persona Benefit Mapping: FEEL, Not State

Self-contained persona targeting. Auto-detects which personas to address from post content.

---

## AUTO-DETECTION

```
CONTENT SIGNAL                                       → PRIMARY PERSONA
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Rankings, crawl budget, indexing, on-page SEO         → SEO Practitioner
Content strategy, editorial calendars, topic clusters → Content Marketer
AI answers, featured snippets, AEO, zero-click       → Digital Strategist
Site speed, Core Web Vitals, technical SEO            → Developer / Tech SEO
Traffic growth, conversions, ROI from organic         → Marketing Manager
Business growth, market share, competitive edge       → Founder / CTO
```

Pick 1-2 per post. The content's scenario naturally resonates with the right personas.

**Load persona priority from CONTEXT.md** (default for SEO: SEO Practitioner > Content Marketer > Digital Strategist > Marketing Manager > Founder)

---

## RULE: FEEL, NOT STATE

**NEVER:** "This benefits SEO teams by..." or "Content marketers will find this useful because..."
**INSTEAD:** Write scenarios that make them FEEL the benefit.

---

## SEO PRACTITIONER (Primary)

**Make them feel:** "I should try this on my next audit"

**Scenarios:**
- Spending hours on keyword research only to target the wrong intent
- A page ranking #1 for 6 months suddenly drops to page 2 with no changes
- Watching a competitor outrank you with thinner content because of better schema
- Manually checking 200 pages for crawl issues when a rule change could fix it
- Getting asked "why did traffic drop?" with no clear answer

**Example (GOOD):**
> I spent a week optimizing 40 blog posts based on keyword difficulty scores. 35 of them barely moved. The 5 that jumped? They weren't targeting the highest-volume keyword. They were the only ones answering the actual question Google was surfacing in AI Overviews.

---

## CONTENT MARKETER

**Make them feel:** "I've been creating content wrong"

**Scenarios:**
- Publishing 4 posts a week but organic traffic is flat
- Writing for keywords instead of topics and wondering why nothing clusters
- Competitor publishes half as much but ranks for twice the keywords
- Spending time on content that gets indexed but never gets impressions

---

## DIGITAL STRATEGIST

**Make them feel:** "The search landscape is shifting and I need to adapt"

**Scenarios:**
- Featured snippets being replaced by AI-generated answers
- Zero-click searches eating into organic traffic
- Clients asking "what's our AEO strategy?" and not having one
- Traditional SEO metrics (rankings, traffic) not telling the full picture anymore

---

## DEVELOPER / TECH SEO

**Make them feel:** "That's a quick win I can implement today"

**Scenarios:**
- Schema markup that's technically valid but not triggering rich results
- Core Web Vitals scores that look fine in lab data but fail in field data
- Crawl budget wasted on parameterized URLs and faceted navigation
- Site migration that killed organic traffic because of redirect chain issues

---

## MARKETING MANAGER

**Make them feel:** "I can show this ROI to my team"

**Scenarios:**
- Organic traffic growing but pipeline/conversions flat
- Paid spend increasing while organic channel is underfunded
- No clear attribution between content effort and business outcomes

---

## FOUNDER / CTO (Minimal Focus)

**Make them feel:** "SEO is a growth lever, not a cost center"

Use sparingly. This is implied by solving everyone else's problems.

---

## HOW TO APPLY

1. Auto-detect 1-2 personas from the content signal table
2. Write the scenario using "you" or "I" language
3. Pain first (80%), resolution (20%)
4. Never label the persona in the post
5. Readers self-identify through the scenario
